Self & Place

project description

The purpose of this project is to use photographs to digitally represent yourself based on your chosen topic. My concept for this project is to show two contrasting photo that represents my “private self”, the person not everyone sees and my “public self”, what everyone else sees me like on social media (instagram).

Concept

The first photo is my private self. I planned to take this picture in a more quiet environment to represent mystery/privacy. I decided to take this picture standing in between an aisle in a public library while holding my laptop in front of me. By standing behind the laptop screen it represents my true self, the real person behind my social media self. I found an aisle with empty shelves on both sides and thought it would be best to represent loneliness/privacy since it’s empty. I also gave the photo a low saturation to bring out the greys and blacks since those colours represents mystery and security. The photo was shot with me standing in the middle. The lines from the selves lead the eye towards the centre where I was standing. It was also shot at eye point of view giving it more of a reality feel. My private self isn’t perfect as it looks through my social media its the reality, it isn’t as simple as it may seem online. It’s all the good and the bad something not everyone gets to see. No one would ever be able to tell who I really was just looking at my online persona and I decided to represent this by showing myself as the main subject behind a computer screen.

My contrasting photo, however, represents my social media self, which is only the good and fun aspects in my life. I portrayed this by going into a more urban/public setting. Since this photo only represents the fun stuff people see through my social media life, I decided to add wings to my back. The wings represent the online persona I’ve created for myself, it also makes it more of a imaginary not reality since everything portrayed on social media isn’t always as it seems. In the photo I’m positioned more towards the left with my head looking back. This photo was shot at a low point of view to make me seem more powerful and in control which is the power that social media lets a person have. I decided to removed the buildings behind me so it would seem like I was looking towards the empty white sky, something unknown/imaginary . The tones for this photo is light so I used the colours blue and white since they represent power, creation and simple.

Process

brainstorming

Figure 3. Brainstorming.

Figure 4. Draft photos.

I started out by creating two mind maps to draught down ideas for my photos. The first mind map is for my private self and the second map is for my public self. Then I took these ideas and went out with a friend to take some experimenting shots. After taking the experimenting shots, I went home and looked over them. I tried to create a concept using the photos I had and explored the ideas that I had in mind.

For the first photo I wanted to keep the idea of me being behind the computer screen to show my true private self and for the second photo I decided I to be in an urban area and add a photo of my hand holding a pencil to make it look like I’m drawing a picture of myself which would represents how my public social media self is a person I’ve created not my true self.

photo selection

Figure 5. Private Self Unedited.

Figure 6. Public Self Images Unedited.

After retaking all my photos I narrowed it down to the final photos that would best represent my idea. From there, I started to individually edit the photos starting with the "private self" photo.

For the first photo, I went into image adjustment and used the hue/saturation settings to lower the saturation down by -50. Next, I used the quick selection tool to select the background of the photo to adjust the brightness and contrast. From there, I select the light on the ceiling and darken it with the lightness adjustment in hue/saturation settings.

For the second photo, after opening the original photo in photoshop, I imported the picture of the seagull and used the quick selection tool to crop the bird. Then using the quick selection tool again I selected only the wings/body of the bird. I copied and pasted the wings to make a total of 4. Using the transformation tool I began to enlarge and shape the wings to my liking and then repeated the process for the rest of the wings. After placing the group of wings behind my back, I used the quick selection tool once again to select my whole body and the wings. Then I copied and paste it onto a new layer that I’ve created, from there I was able to use the cloning tool to photoshop out the buildings behind me from the original photo. Finally I selected the background layer and used the hue/saturation tool in image adjustment to increase the saturation and turn up the lightness.

conclusion

The outcome of this digital image design project was excellent, the photos come out as I planned in both the print and digital version. Even though the outcome of this was great, I believe the photos can still be further refined to produce an even better result especially the wings in the second photo.
